Alteration form

https://linked.data.gov.au/def/alteration-form

Metadata

definitionCompilation of terms that described geometry (i.e. the shape, distribution and configuration) of alteration in rocks and minerals as observed at the macro-, meso- and microscopic scale, and applying to the specific area of observation. Alteration in this context is defined as a modification of the original lithology caused by meteoric, connate, metamorphic, or magmatic-derived fluids, excluding the weathering environment, mineralization and direct magmatic or metamorphic processes. Definitions are largely taken from Pirajno (2009) and the Glossary of geology (Neuendorf et al., 2011).
history noteDerived from GSWA's WAROX database's table [GEOLOGY].[WAROX.REF].[AltForm], and expanded to include additional terms. Definitions compiled from: 1) Neuendorf, KKE, Mehl Jr, JP and Jackson, JA (editors) 2011, Glossary of geology (5th edition, revised): American Geosciences Institute, Alexandria, Virginia, USA, 800p. 2) Pirajno, F 2009, Hydrothermal processes and mineral systems: Springer-Verlag, 1250p. 3) Thompson, JFH, Thompson, AJB and Allen, RL 1996, Atlas of alteration: A field and petrographic guide to hydrothermal alteration minerals, St. John's, Nfld: Geological Association of Canada, Mineral Deposits Division, 119p.
