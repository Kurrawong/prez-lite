Sample data quality management

https://linked.data.gov.au/def/sample-data-quality-management

Metadata

definitionThe types of physical samples that are used to undertake quality assurance (QA) and quality control (QC) checks at all stages of the sampling, preparation and analytical processes, and/or to retain representative material for future reference. Note that QA and QC are often used collectively as a single term, but are in fact distinct procedures: QA 'refers to defect prevention', i.e. ‘the protocols and procedures that ensure that sampling and assaying is completed to the required quality’, whereas QC 'refers to defect detection', i.e. ‘the use of control samples and statistical analysis to ensure that assay results are reliable’ (Graindorge, 2010, p. 112; https://www.usgs.gov/data-management/manage-quality). This vocabulary thus also encapsulates terminology covering sample representability.

history noteQA and QC details must be outlined as part of the JORC Table 1 requirements when reporting Exploration Results, Mineral Resource and Ore Reserve Estimates in Australia, particularly to the Australian Stock Exchange; other exchanges and other CRIRSCO reporting codes have similar rulings.
